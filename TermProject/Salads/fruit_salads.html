Fruit Salads


Refreshing Fruit Salad

This simple fruit salad is a quick, healthy dessert. In summer, try freezing this in cups with an upside down spoon in the center. Take off the cup and it’s a refreshing, healthy after-school or Shabbos afternoon treat.

Difficulty: Easy
Diet: Vegetarian , Vegan , Pescetarian , Gluten Free , Low Fat
Cooking and Prep: 10 min
Serves: 6
Allergy: No Allergens
Author: Kosher.com/ Dining In

Ingredients

  • 1 (16-ounce) container strawberries in sauce
  • 2 small cans Gefen Mandarin Oranges
  • 2 cans Gefen Pineapple Tidbits
  • 6 to 8 yellow apples
  • 1 cup green grapes
  • 1 cup red grapes
  • 2 kiwifruit
  • 1 banana, optional

Prepare the Fruit Salad

  1. Pour container of strawberries with the sauce into large bowl.
  2. Drain mandarin oranges and pineapple and add to the strawberries.
  3. Dice apples and add to bowl. Add grapes and toss gently.
  4. Immediately before serving, peel and dice kiwis and bananas, and add to salad.

Fruit Salad with Strawberry Coulis

The crunchy nuts, smooth strawberry coulis, and fresh fruit are three components that, combined, build to a lovely fruit salad crescendo. This fruit salad can serve either as a healthful starter course or a light, refreshing dessert. Aim for a combination of at least four different types of fruits.

Difficulty: Medium
Diet: Vegetarian , Pescetarian
Cooking and Prep: 1.5 hour
Serves: 8
Allergy: contains NUTS
Author: Whisk by Ami Magazine

Ingredients

Strawberry Coulis

  • 1 cup strawberries, fresh or frozen
  • 1/2 cup orange juice
  • 1/2 cup confectioners’ sugar
  • 3 tablespoons sugar
  • 1 teaspoon Gefen Vanilla Extract
  • 1 tablespoon lemon or lime juice
  • 1 tablespoon Bartenura Balsamic Vinegar

Nut Crunch Topping

  • 1/2 cup brown sugar
  • 1 and 1/2 cups Rice Krispies
  • 4 ounces (1/2 cup) trans fat-free margarine (use soy-free, if needed)
  • 1 cup walnuts or filberts, chopped

Fruit Salad

  • 2 cups strawberries, diced
  • 2 cups blueberries
  • 2 cups mango, peeled and diced
  • 2 cups kiwis, peeled and diced
  • pomegranate seeds, for sprinkling

Prepare the Coulis

  1. In a food processor, puree all the ingredients until smooth. Refrigerate until ready to serve.

Prepare the Nut Crunch

  1. Preheat oven to 350 degrees Fahrenheit.
  2. In a roasting pan, combine the following ingredients and bake, uncovered, for 10 minutes.:
    • brown sugar
    • Rice Krispies
    • margarine
    • walnuts
  3. Remove from the oven and stir well to blend the flavors. Allow to cool. Store in an airtight container until ready to serve.

Serve

  1. In a large bowl, combine the fruits.
  2. Scoop the fruit salad into individual serving bowls. Spoon the strawberry coulis over the fruit salad, then spoon the nut crunch on top and serve immediately.

Note: Any leftover nut crunch can be frozen.
Tip: For added effect and flavor, sprinkle pomegranate seeds on top of the fruit salad before serving.


Peach Strawberry Salad

Here's a salad that screams summer. With a strawberry puree dressing, fresh peach slices and crunchy nuts, this sweet salad is like a burst of sunshine on a plate.

Difficulty: Easy
Diet: Vegetarian , Vegan , Pescetarian , Gluten Free
Cooking and Prep: 10 min
Serves: 8
Allergy: contains NUTS
Author: Kosher.com/ Dining In

Ingredients

  • 10 ounces frozen strawberries, thawed
  • 1 tablespoon raspberry vinegar
  • 8 medium-size ripe peaches
  • 3 tablespoons lemon juice
  • lettuce, for serving
  • 1/4 cup sliced almonds, toasted
  • fresh strawberries, for garnish (optional)

Prepare the Dressing

  1. Place thawed strawberries in blender and blend until smooth. Press puree through a strainer to remove “seeds.” Add raspberry vinegar to puree; stir well and set aside.

Prepare the Peaches

  1. Cut peaches in half lengthwise and remove pits. Slice into quarter- inch-thick slices, lengthwise, leaving slices attached half and inch at stem end (make cuts from bottom ends).
  2. Brush peaches with lemon juice.

Assemble

  1. Arrange lettuce leaves on eight salad plates decoratively. Arrange peaches evenly over lettuce (two peach halves per plate), letting slices fan out slightly.
  2. Drizzle each salad with two tablespoons strawberry puree and sprinkle with toasted almonds. Garnish with fresh strawberries, if desired. Serve immediately.
